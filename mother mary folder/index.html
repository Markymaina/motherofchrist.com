About Mary

Mary, a young woman from Nazareth, was chosen by God to be the mother of Jesus Christ. Her life is a testament to faith and obedience. When the angel Gabriel appeared to her, announcing that she would conceive by the Holy Spirit, she responded with unwavering trust: “I am the handmaid of the Lord; let it be done to me according to your word.” This moment marked the beginning of salvation history.

Theological Significance

Mary is often referred to as the "Mother of God," a title that underscores her unique role in the divine plan. This doctrine affirms that Jesus is both fully divine and fully human. Through her acceptance of God's will, Mary exemplifies perfect obedience and faithfulness, making her a central figure in Christianity.

Veneration

Throughout history, Mary has been venerated by millions. The Solemnity of Mary, Mother of God on January 1 celebrates her divine motherhood. Devotions such as the Rosary and various prayers highlight her role as intercessor. She is seen as a compassionate mother who cares for all believers.

Miracles Associated with Mary

Mary's presence has been associated with numerous miraculous events throughout history. One of the most significant apparitions occurred in Fatima, Portugal, in 1917. Here, she appeared to three shepherd children—Lucia Santos and her cousins Jacinta and Francisco Marto—urging them to pray for peace and conversion.

The Miracle of the Sun took place on October 13, 1917, where thousands witnessed the sun appearing to dance in the sky. This event not only affirmed Mary's presence but also strengthened faith among those who witnessed it.

Another notable apparition occurred at Lourdes in 1858 when Mary appeared to Bernadette Soubirous. The spring that emerged from this site has been attributed to miraculous healings over the years.
